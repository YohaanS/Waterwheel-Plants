Waterwheel Plant - Ameya Vyas, Yohaan Singla

Did you know that the Waterwheel Plant is one of the only plant species that can rapidly move?
The Waterwheel plant(Aldrovanda Vesiculosa) is part of the Kingdom Plante, Order Caryophyllales, Family Droseraceae, and Genus Aldrovanda(The Waterwheel Plant is the only living species in the Genus).
The Waterwheel Plant is a rootless floating plant with leaves arranged in circular patterns attached to the long stem.
The arrangement of the leaves gives the Waterwheel Plant its name.

Habitat

To begin with, we’ll talk about the Waterwheel Plant’s habitat.
The Waterwheel Plant was found in 50 locations spread across Asia, Africa, Europe, and Australia.
There are also areas in the Eastern U.S. where the Waterwheel Plant lives and it might be invasive there.
The Waterwheel Plant lives in warm, clean shallow water areas that receive lots of sunlight.
The water where the Waterwheel Plant lives doesn't have very many nutrients, so it eats underwater invertebrates to get its nutrients.

Preying mecinesem and prey

Additionally, the Waterwheel Plant has a snap-trap on the end of its leaves.
These traps snap shut when prey touches hairs on the Waterwheel Plant.
Small bristles keep debris away from the snap trap.
The Waterwheel Plant eats underwater invertebrates.
These include Mosquito Larvae, Eelworms, Daphnia, and more.

Conclution

In conclusion, the Waterwheel Plant is a carnivorous plant that resides underwater.
The Waterwheel Plant was found in Africa, Australia, Europe, Asia, and the Eastern U.S.
The Waterwheel Plant feeds on various invertebrates in water.
